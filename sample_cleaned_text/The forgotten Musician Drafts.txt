How to write microfiction:
1. Start with a theme/prompt.
Theme: The Forgotten Musician
People remember the art but often forget the artist(Music:Peace, Musician: The ones
who brought peace).
2. Time to reflect on and include the details. Who is/are your main character(s)? What is their
conflict? What happens to them? Where does your story happen? Only because your story is
very short does not mean you should not try to have vibrant characters in memorable settings!
Main character: Levi,a Paradis soldier, is a stoic, introvert, cleanliness freak person. He
does not have a biological family, but his military squad serves as his family to him.
Conflict: constant conditions of war and bloodshed, Fighting for freedom of Paradis
against the Marley nation who wants to conquer the Paradi land for its resources. At the same
time, the people of Paradis live in constant fear, hoping the war never reaches their towns.
What happens: The war broke down on the land of Paradis. Levi loses many mates over
multiple battles. Despite all this, he did not shed a tear. After Paradis secured victory, he finally
shed the tears telling all his martyr comrades that their death was not in vain. In later years, the
newer generation views him as any other human being, unaware of the sacrifices he made for
their freedom. As this is set to occur in 850, the limited media coverage of heroes of the war.
Eventually he hands out candies sitting on a wheelchair to the children of the affected area, with
them unaware of who the man is. Having done so much of his own land, saw the deaths of his
closed comrades, still he was unknown for many. History remembered the war but not the men
who fought it.
Where: At the border of Paradis Island
3. Include all details and compose your first draft. Tell the story, adding in more details that you
think are important. Do not worry about word count as yet.
Levi Ackerman, a Paradis soldier, was a stoic, introverted, obsessively clean person; he always
kept his blades clean, and was disgusted when blood dripped over them. He did not have a
biological family, but the people he fought beside, ate with, and trusted his life with, his military
squad served as his family to him.
In the year 850, the presence of war was unavoidable. The Marleyan nation wanted to conquer
the lands of Paradis Island for its rich natural resources and strategic location. Conditions of war
and bloodshed were a common sight. The people of Paradis were in constant fear hoping the
war would never reach them.
Levi, the captain of his squad, was acting as a shield between the enemies and the civilians
who slept in fear, standing at the war forefront. His squad consisted of the finest of the soldiers
with years of intense combat. But one by one, he saw his comrades give their lives for their
land, fighting till their last breath. He did not shed any tears, for he knew he had to stay strong
for his nation.

The war ended with the victory of Paradis. Levi finally let a tear roll down his cheeks, saluting
his martyred comrades, conveying that their deaths were not in vain.
With each passing year, the stories of the war grew quieter.
Months passed.
Levi, sitting in a wheelchair, spent his days in the affected areas, handing out candies to the
children. The laughter of the children echoed through the streets which were once filled with
blood and screams. They thanked him, unaware of who he was. They saw him only as a quiet
old man.
Levi watched them with a faint smile. Not letting them know who he is, for he does not need any
recognition. Levi faded into silence, leaving behind a world that no longer needed to remember
his name.
4. In the second draft, edit and let go of all redundant words. Aim for clarity and try to convey
your central idea without rambling.

Island for rich resources.

breath, becoming martyrs. No tears.

5. In the third draft, read all your sentences again. Polish each sentence. Make sure that each
word is conveying something—a feeling, an atmosphere, a detail. Your first and your last
sentences should receive extra attention
Levi was a stoic Paradis soldier; his squad was all he had.
War was unavoidable. Levi saw his comrades die.
Paradis won. A tear fell as Levi saluted the dead.
Months passed, and the stories of the war grew quieter.

6. Now add a suitable title. It should contribute as much to the story as every sentence you have
just perfected!
The Peace Composer
7. Step back and read the piece as a reader. Does it make sense to a reader who is not aware
of the background or the context of the story/author? Does this piece strike a chord, making you
want to read the writer more often? Is yes, you are good to go. If you are confused, send the
draft to someone whose opinion you trust. Listen to critical feedback and work on changing what
did not work.

8. You are at your fourth draft now- and most probably, your microfiction is complete.
The Peace Composer
In the war's dark orchestra, Levi stood unbroken, his squad the only harmony he knew. One by
one, their notes fell silent. Peace followed.
Years later, in a wheelchair, he offered candies to laughing children who knew only sunshine,
not storms.
They danced to peace’s melody, long past needing the one who wrote it.
